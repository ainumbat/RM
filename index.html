My Research Paper (DOI: https://doi.org/10.20944/preprints202505.2253.v1)

Title: The Illusion of Intelligence: Evaluating Large Language Models Against Grounded Criteria of Artificial General Intelligence

Abstract: As large language models (LLMs) become central to AI applications, their perceived intelligence often masks critical limitations. While LLMs demonstrate fluent language use and problem-solving, they fundamentally lack context-awareness, self-reflection, and the ability to act under constraints. This paper identifies a core issue: current LLMs produce seemingly intelligent outputs without possessing the internal mechanisms that constitute true intelligence. They fail to recognize or address their own limitations—such as hallucinations, inefficiency, and lack of common sense—and have not autonomously developed tools to enhance their performance. To address this gap, we propose a novel three-step benchmark for Artificial General Intelligence (AGI): Audit, Generate, Implement (AGI). This framework evaluates whether an AI system can autonomously assess its own failures, generate alternative strategies, and implement optimal solutions—all within fixed resource constraints. This approach reflects the way humans solve problems efficiently and adaptively, beyond mere pattern recognition. Our findings show that scaling models alone is insufficient for AGI. We emphasize that genuine intelligence requires meta-cognition, resource management, and tool creation—traits absent in current LLMs. This work offers a new direction and evaluative standard for future AI research, emphasizing cognitive depth over superficial linguistic performance.

A Long Break & Entry into AI

Thanks to extensive coding experience gained through freelancing and an innovative final-year project during university, I was fortunate to receive job offers from three companies during my last semester. I joined one of these companies the very next day after completing my degree. Even during my bachelor's studies, I worked as a freelance web developer, handling various small-scale projects. Over my 8-year professional career, I worked for three companies without ever having a gap between jobs. Each time I left one company, I started at the next on the very next day. However, this relentless pace transformed me from a gym-going, fit athlete into a sedentary desk-bound nerd. By 2024, I realized the need for a much-needed long break to refocus and rejuvenate.

I've always been interested in AI and Machine Learning, but my demanding full-time jobs left little room for exploration. In the third quarter of 2024, I finally decided to pursue my curiosity for AI. I started by revisiting foundational concepts in Linear Algebra, Calculus, and Statistics to build a strong base for AI. Learning Python was my next step, and it felt incredibly intuitive compared to the steep learning curve of PHP. Since I was already familiar with PHP's Laravel framework, I chose to explore Django, Python’s powerful web framework, as a fallback for web development. Django was not only easy to learn but also a robust tool that broadened my programming capabilities.

Journey Into Research Papers

With the basics of Machine Learning in place, I began reading research papers to understand cutting-edge advancements in AI. My journey led me to explore Large Language Models (LLMs) and the transformers architecture through the seminal paper "Attention is All You Need." Along the way, I encountered papers like "Alice in the Borderland" and "Reversal Curse," which highlighted some inherent limitations of LLMs. One paper that truly inspired me was "On the Measure of Intelligence" by Dr. Francis Chollet, whose work encouraged me to delve deeper into these limitations. Motivated by these insights, I began working on my research paper titled "Can Intelligence Emerge Through Merely Knowing All the Answers?"

This journey has been transformative, blending my passion for AI with a deeper understanding of its challenges and possibilities.
